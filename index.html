About the Double Slit Experiment

The double slit experiment demonstrates the wave-particle duality of quantum mechanics. When particles like electrons pass through two slits, they create an interference pattern as if they were waves, even when fired one at a time.

This suggests that each particle somehow explores both paths simultaneously, interfering with itself. As Richard Feynman noted, this experiment encapsulates the fundamental strangeness of quantum mechanics.
